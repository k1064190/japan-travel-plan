Stage 7

Replaced abstract hotel placeholders with the two actual booked hotels (Sugata Shinsaibashi and Ebisuhigashi), correcting Google Maps direction origins for Days 1–5.

Named Hotels

Why

Until this stage, accommodations were represented by abstract IDs like namba_hotel. The two actually-booked hotels occupy different locations for nights 1–2 vs. 3–4. Without correcting the data, the Google Maps direction-link origins would point at the wrong starting coordinates, undermining the transit time credibility restored in Stage 6.

What

  • Three new place entries in places.json: sugata_hotel (Sugata Hotel Osaka Shinsaibashi, Marriott Series), ebisuhigashi_hotel (2-1-24 Ebisuhigashi, Naniwa-ku), ebisuhigashi_hotel_checkout (Day 5 utility stop)
  • Itinerary hotel references replaced: Day 1 check-in → sugata_hotel; Day 2 first-stop transit origin and last stop → sugata_hotel; Day 3 first-stop origin and check-in stop → ebisuhigashi_hotel; Day 4 → ebisuhigashi_hotel; Day 5 first stop → ebisuhigashi_hotel_checkout
  • Walking time corrected: Day 1 / dotonbori 8 → 12 min (Sugata to Dotonbori is ~900 m, up from the old ~300 m namba_hotel baseline)

How

A one-shot /tmp/patch-hotels.mjs script patched both places.json and itinerary.json consistently in a single run. Sugata coordinates came from the user-supplied Google Maps URL (34.6766637, 135.504813). Ebisuhigashi coordinates were estimated from the street address (~1–2 blocks east of Tsutenkaku, 34.6515, 135.508) pending confirmation. Validation and walk-time verification both passed after the walking-time correction.

Code Locations

  • data/places.json — sugata_hotel, ebisuhigashi_hotel, ebisuhigashi_hotel_checkout added
  • data/itinerary.json — Day 1–5 stops and transit_from_prev.origin_place_id updated

Retrospective

The origin_place_id field introduced in Stage 6 was exactly the right hook for this scenario: adding two real hotels required zero UI code changes. The validate-data and verify-walk scripts immediately flagged the Sugata→Dotonbori walking time as too short, making the correction automatic to discover.
