Stage 10

Sidebar “Nearby Candidates” section with rating-sorted cards below itinerary stops, plus food-only and far-candidates filter toggles shared with the map layer.

Sidebar Candidates

Why

Stage 8/9 exposed alt candidates and restaurants as map markers, but the sidebar itinerary tab showed none of them. Small markers are hard to tap precisely on a phone screen, and scanning ratings requires looking at each marker individually. Showing candidates as cards in the sidebar, sorted by rating, lets users instantly see which place is most popular and provides a larger tap target.

What

  • Sidebar “Nearby Candidates” section below the itinerary stop list: cards sorted by rating descending (unrated entries appended alphabetically); card click → selectAlt(id); each card shows emoji + name + 3 tags + inline rating
  • Two filter toggles (inline checkboxes): Food only (isFoodPlace — rating field present OR food-keyword tag regex match); Show far candidates (expands radius from ALT_PROXIMITY_KM 2 km to ALT_FAR_KM 8 km to surface intentionally-distant entries like Tsuruhashi Korea Town)
  • Map markers and sidebar cards share the same filter state — one toggle updates both

How

Two booleans added to state: filterFood and showFarCandidates. altCandidatesForDay(day, opts) signature extended with opts.foodOnly and opts.far; both renderDay and renderSidebar call it with the same options object. renderSidebar emits a candidateSection block after the stops <ul>, sorted by rating descending. Toggle change events update state then call both renderDay and renderSidebar to keep map and sidebar in sync.

Code Locations

  • assets/app.js:137-172 (state fields, ALT_PROXIMITY_KM/ALT_FAR_KM, FOOD_TAG_RE, isFoodPlace)
  • assets/app.js:180-205 (altCandidatesForDay opts parameter)
  • assets/app.js:247-251 (renderDay passing state options)
  • assets/app.js:347-471 (renderSidebar candidate section + toggle handlers)

Retrospective

Adding an opts parameter to altCandidatesForDay was all that was needed for both callers to support toggles from a single source of truth. The isFoodPlace heuristic works well in practice: entries with a rating field are almost exclusively restaurants. Day 1’s top card was Yakiniku M (Tabelog 3.78), Day 2’s was Yamamoto Menzo (3.92).
